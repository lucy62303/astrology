A brief history

Astrology has an ancient and diverse history that spans across various cultures and civilizations. Its origins can be traced back to ancient Mesopotamia, where the Babylonians developed a system of celestial observations and interpretations around 2,000 BCE. Initially used to predict natural phenomena and divine omens, these practices evolved into a more sophisticated system that linked celestial patterns with human destinies. Babylonian astrology spread to other cultures, such as Egypt and Greece, where it merged with local beliefs and traditions. In Greece, figures like Ptolemy played a pivotal role in refining and codifying astrological principles in the 2nd century CE, laying the foundation for Western astrology.
During the medieval and Renaissance periods, astrology gained substantial prominence in Europe. It became intertwined with religious, philosophical, and political ideologies, and was considered a respected science alongside astronomy. Astrology was used to advise rulers, guide medical practices, and offer insights into individuals' personalities and life events. However, with the advent of the Scientific Revolution in the 17th century, empirical observation and rational inquiry began to challenge astrology's credibility. As science advanced, astrology gradually lost its status as a mainstream scientific discipline.
Despite its decline in scientific recognition, astrology persisted as a popular cultural practice. In the 19th and 20th centuries, various movements sought to revive and reformulate astrological concepts. The New Age movement, for instance, reintroduced astrology as a tool for self-discovery, spirituality, and personal growth. In the 20th century, psychological astrology emerged, focusing on the symbolic and archetypal interpretations of astrological symbols. Today, astrology continues to evolve in the digital age, with online platforms and social media facilitating its widespread accessibility. While many individuals engage with astrology for entertainment and introspection, its scientific validity remains a subject of ongoing debate within both the astrological and scientific communities.
